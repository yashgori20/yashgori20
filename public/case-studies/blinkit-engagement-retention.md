# Engagement & Retention Strategy - Blinkit [B2C Product]

Blinkit is a hyperlocal grocery delivery platform that strives to provide it’s users with instant access to everyday items that they need the most. They aim to provide their users with items across a plethora of categories ranging from groceries, fruits & vegetables, stationary, home decor and countless others and delivering products from all these categories right in minutes. So the user does not have to step outside their home ever and in the click of a button, they can get all their products delivered right to their doorsteps.

Their main motive is to help users out with their daily needs without having them to worry about squeezing time for such chores in the middle of their day. They operate with a dark store model where each dark store is at maximum a distance of 2km from the delivery point, thereby being able to fulfill their promise of being able to deliver the products right in under 10 minutes [Also ensures in return the safety of the delivery executives since they would be having ample time to deliver the product]. 

With the use of data analytics and machine learning algorithms, they are able to precisely pinpoint as to what products across which categories are required the most in an area. Thus, this helps them to stock up their dark stores accordingly and in turn the user will be able to fulfill all their needs right in minutes and won’t have to face issues such as products being out of stock.

With Blinkit, get ready for a magical adventure where your favorite stuff appears at your door in the blink of an eye! We're mixing up tech, data, and a bunch of cool stores to totally shake up online shopping. It's all about making things super-fast and easy, like nothing you've ever seen before! 

### Core Value Proposition:

1. **Instant Delivery**: Blinkit offers lightning-fast deliveries, often within minutes of placing an order. This speed sets them apart in the e-commerce world, providing customers with immediate gratification.
2. **Vast Partner Network**: Blinkit has built a robust network of dark stores across localities, ensuring a wide range of products and options for users to choose from. This expansive alliance enhances convenience and product availability.
3. **Seamless Technology Integration**: By seamlessly integrating technology into their platform, Blinkit simplifies the ordering process, making it user-friendly and efficient. This includes user-friendly apps for a smooth user experience.
4. **Data-Driven Personalization**: Blinkit leverages data sciences to understand user preferences and behavior. This allows them to offer personalized recommendations and a tailored shopping experience, increasing user satisfaction.
5. **Unparalleled Convenience**: Blinkit's core value proposition revolves around convenience. With the combination of instant deliveries, a vast product selection, and user-friendly technology, Blinkit aims to redefine how users perceive and experience online shopping, making it more convenient than ever before.
6. **Reliable Quality Assurance**: Ensure that products delivered through Blinkit meet high-quality standards, providing users with peace of mind about the products they receive.
7. **Availability**: Offer around-the-clock service to cater to users' needs at any time, making shopping more flexible and accessible. But nowadays they have stopped working from 1 AM - 5 AM in most of the major metro cities. 
8. **Eco-Friendly Delivery Options**: Provide eco-conscious delivery choices, such as sustainable packaging, to attract environmentally conscious users.
9. **Customer Support Excellence**: Provide exceptional customer support to address any queries or issues promptly, ensuring a positive overall experience.
10. **Safety and Security**: Emphasize safety and security measures for both users and delivery executives to build trust and ensure a safe shopping experience.

### How does a user experience the core value prop repeatedly?

1. **Quick Deliveries**: Users could place orders through the Blinkit platform and receive their products within minutes [they even have a tagline which says “India’s last minute delivery app”], which is significantly faster than other platforms. This instant gratification aspect is a key part of their value proposition.
2. **User-Friendly App**: Blinkit's app was designed to be user-friendly, allowing users to easily browse products, place orders, and track deliveries. The simplicity and efficiency of the platform contribute to the convenience factor.
3. **Wide Product Selection**: Blinkit established a network of dark stores, ensuring a diverse range of products was available for users to choose from. This extensive selection allowed users to repeatedly find what they needed through Blinkit.
4. **Personalization**: Blinkit leveraged data sciences to provide personalized product recommendations and offers to users based on their past purchases and browsing history. This personal touch enhanced the user experience and encouraged repeat usage. Also, via these data insights, they are able to stock up their dark stores well in advance to ensure that needs of all users are satisfied.
5. **Prompt Customer Support**: Users experiencing any issues with their orders or deliveries could reach out to Blinkit's customer support team for quick resolution. Effective customer support is crucial in maintaining trust and ensuring a positive experience.
6. **Feedback and Improvement**: Blinkit might have actively sought user feedback to improve their services continuously. This iterative approach ensures that they meet customer expectations and enhance their core value proposition over time.
7. **Cost Savings and Offers:** Users can benefit from competitive prices, discounts, and promotional offers available through the application, allowing them to save money on their grocery purchases.

### Natural Frequency of the Core Product

1. Casual Users: The natural frequency of a casual user for Blinkit is 1-2 orders a month thereby leading to use the app once in a month and at times even less frequently than that. These users do not order very frequently and thus at times only 1 order a month.
2. Core Users: The natural frequency of a core user for Blinkit is 1-2 orders a week thereby leading to use the app on a weekly basis mainly. These users rely on Blinkit mainly to get their weekly grocery supplies or at times getting something urgently when they need a specific product. These users are a good advocate of the product who can serve as a good referrer to acquire more users.
3. Power Users: The natural frequency of a power user for Blinkit is 2 orders a day thereby leading to use the app several times a week. These users rely on Blinkit for all their day-to-day needs.

### Define - Who is an active user of the product?

Any user who orders once in every 15 days [2 orders in a month] can be considered as an active user. 

- Casual Users: A user who orders 1-2 times a month can be considered as a casual user.
- Core Users: A user who orders 1-2 times a week can be considered as a core user.
- Power Users: A user who orders 1-2 times in a day can be considered as a power user.

## Retention

### Bird’s Eye View

- What is your current retention rate in terms of users?
 - The current retention rate is at an astounding 69%. [[Source](https://www.notion.so/1d16cd6e47b542e1b17f256a44ee906e?pvs=21)]. Last year in H1 2022, it was around 65% but now they have increased retention by 4%.
- At what time period does your retention curve flatten?
 - As per Blinkit’s Growth Team - Bharti [Associate Director - Product Growth], the retention curve flattens mainly after successful product deliveries. A successful product delivery is one where there are no items missing, no product spillage and nothing has to be rescheduled & the user in most likelihood gives a 4+ star rating for the order. This as a metric can be achieved only when the user’s JTBD has been satisfied. The time period to achieve this is around 2 months, with a minimum of 20-25 orders [weekly 2-3 orders on an average] inorder to cement the entire experience through consecutive deliveries and to make the user rely on Blinkit for all their needs.

### Microscopic View

1. Which ICP’s drive the best retention?
The core and the power users drive the best retention since they to order very frequently and also across a breadth of categories. As per my ICP segmentation, this is ICP 1 mainly consisting of bachelors, working professionals & the millenial users. The age-group that mainly drives retention is in the bracket of 25-35. [[Source](https://www.exchange4media.com/digital-news/zepto-clocks-946-growth-in-4-months-between-dec-21-march-22-bobble-ai-report-120258.html)]
2. Which channels drive the best retention?
- Recommendations: Tailoring product recommendations based on data and user insights allows for a personalized user experience within the app.
- Push Notifications: Push Notifications helps to notify the users of any current offers running and also can target the core & power users at various times throughout the day with various campaigns.
- Social Media [SM]: A good SM profile helps to build a loyal customer base around the app. Also, a very hard truth, but in 2023 SM also acts as a Customer Support channel much efficiently as compared to traditional support channels. This in-turn keeps the users happy as well with instant resolution and helps the brand make a loyal customer base.
- App Notifications: This channel helps to target the core & power users precisely and thus pushing them to order via Blinkit.

1. What sub-features or sub-products drive the best retention?
 
 Currently Blinkit has grouped all of it’s sub-products as categories like Vegetables & Fruits, Munchies, Juices & Pan Corner to name a few. The categories that drive the best retention are Munchies, Juices, Pan Corner & Health and Wellness products. As per an insight discovered during the user interviews, a lot of users still prefer to buy Vegetables & Fruits from a local vendor since the quality is always top notch. Many times perishable products like Vegetables & Fruits from Blinkit are rotten or have some marks on them, hence hampering the overall experience. Apart from this Blinkit has started a new Printing Service [On-Demand Printout Service], where the users can place an order to print any document and it will be delivered to them within minutes. 
 
 As of now this service is only live in very few regions like Delhi - NCR, so there is no data available online regarding the retention of users using this service.
 
 The features that are driving retention are mainly:
 
 - Instant Deliveries
 - Wide variety of products available
 - Round the clock deliveries - Most of Tier 1 Cities
 - Top quality of products
 - Free goodies - This is only for casual users to help them convert to core users.
 

 

## Define

1. What are the top reasons your user's churn?
- Voluntary Churn [Reasons of Churn which can be solved for by Blinkit]
 - Pricing: Users may churn out if the product listing on Blinkit is more expensive as compared to it’s competitors like Zepto & Dunzo.
 - Competitor Service: Users may churn out if they find better core value prop in using any of Blinkit’s competitors. Example:
 - Buying a subscription plan of any of the competitors [Swiggy One] thereby availing Free Delivery.
 - If a competitor [Zepto] delivers faster in a certain location as compared to Blinkit. The user will order more from Zepto in this case since in the end one of the main JTBD for the users is to deliver their orders quickly.
 - Customer Support not able to provide a satisfactory resolution to the query.
 - Deliveries not being completed on time consecutively and always being delayed.
 - Product quality [especially of perishable goods] not upto the mark.
 - Some items in the order are missing or some wrong items are sent.
 - Users may even churn out if their needs change and thus the products they are looking for are currently not listed on the app.
 - User Experience: If the app shows errors very often or there are some technical glitches with the payment gateways as well, then the users may churn out. As all these small experiences lead to an overall bad user experience.
- Involuntary Churn
 - Relocation: If the user has relocated to another city or area where Blinkit is not serviceable, then the user will churn out.
 - Users shift to traditional buying patterns - buying in bulk from DMart, buying from local vendors
 - Regulatory Changes: Changes in government regulations, local laws, or political instability can affect the availability and legality of grocery-delivering services in certain areas. But this is a pretty rare scenario to occur.
 - Economic Downturn: Certain financial hardships may force those categories of users who relied on Blinkit for their indulgent needs and not basic grocery needs. These users will have to cut back on their spending and thereby will churn out of the app.
 - Disruption in Supply Chain: Events like natural disasters or union strikes may lead to disruption of supply chain. Thus, Blinkit will not be able to maintain the inventory and the users could churn out henceforth.
 
 1. What are the negative actions you would look for?
 - Increase in Cart Abandonment
 - Reduction in order frequency [Specific to that ICP and reduced as compared to the general natural frequency usage of the app]
 - Removal of saved addresses
 - Removal of card details or deleting UPI linked accounts.
 - Given bad reviews for deliveries [might be for late deliveries, missing or wrong items delivered, too much of calling by delivery executive to locate the address etc]
 - Deleting the app itself
 - Reduced AOV of orders as compared to the prior orders
 - Number of times the app has been opened in a week or month has reduced overall
 - Increase in Customer Support Tickets [For refund due to missing/wrong items, cancelled orders due to last minute availability]
