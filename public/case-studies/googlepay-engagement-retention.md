# Google Pay

## 1. Understanding Google Pay

### A. What is the Core Value Proposition(CVP) of Google Pay? : The core value proposition of Google Pay lies in offering users a seamless and secure platform for digital payments. It enables users to:

1. **Convenience:** Make fast and easy payments using their smartphones, reducing the need for physical cards or cash.
2. **Security:** Ensure secure transactions through advanced encryption and authentication measures, instilling trust among users.
3. **Versatility:** Access a versatile platform that not only supports peer-to-peer transactions but also facilitates online and in-store purchases.
4. **Integration with Google Services:** Benefit from integration with other Google services, enhancing the overall user experience.
5. **Rewards and Offers:** Enjoy personalized rewards, cashback, and discounts, incentivizing users to choose Google Pay for their transactions.

### E. What is the Best Engagement Framework for Google Pay? :

| Metric | Definition | Application to Google Pay |

## 2. Define:

## A. Who is an Active user on Google Pay?

An active user is someone who performs a core action repeatedly that aligns with the CVP of the product.

In the case of Google Pay, the core action that aligns with the CVP is making a digital transaction.

 An **active user** on Gpay can be defined as someone who **makes a digital transaction**. 

this transaction can be:

- using it for UPI payments,
- making their bill payments (utilities, recharges, rents, education fees, subscriptions)
- making bank transfers.
- taking loans from lenders integrated with Google Pay and paying them back on a monthly basis for the stipulated tenure
- using it to make credit card payments
- using it to make recurring deposits, buy insurance, and pay taxes to municipal corporations.

### ii. What are the negative user actions to look for?

1. **Continuous Decrease in Transaction Frequency:** A sudden drop in the frequency of transactions may signal disengagement and potential churn.
2. **Reduced App Openings:** A decline in the frequency of opening the Google Pay app might indicate decreasing interest or satisfaction.
3. **Ignored Push Notifications:** If users consistently ignore or disable push notifications, especially those related to exclusive deals, cashback, or reminders, it may suggest a lack of interest in the platform.
4. **Unsubscribing from Communication Channels:** Users opting out of newsletters, promotional emails, or other communication channels can be a strong indicator of waning interest and potential churn.
5. **Low Participation in Rewards Programs:** Users who were previously active in rewards programs but show a decline in participation may be losing interest in the incentives provided by Google Pay.
6. **Negative Feedback or Reviews:** An increase in negative comments regarding functionality, customer support, or rewards can be a warning sign.
7. **Increased Support Tickets:** A surge in customer support tickets related to issues or concerns, indicating a rise in user dissatisfaction
8. **Extended Periods of Inactivity:** Users who remain inactive on the app for extended periods, especially after being active users, may be at risk of churning.
9. **Complaints about Transaction Failures:** Frequent complaints about transaction failures or issues with payment processing may indicate frustration and dissatisfaction.
10. **Lowering CSAT score:** A decline in the overall satisfaction score provided by users through customer satisfaction (CSAT) surveys.
11. **Unresolved Issues:** Users expressing dissatisfaction with the resolution of their issues or concerns by Google Pay support.
12. **Comparative Feedback:** Users provide feedback indicating that they find better service or features in competing payment apps.
